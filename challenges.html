The 500 Barefoot

On June 1 2021, Sav Holmes and her friend Asha Tremblay set out to run 500KMs barefoot for a leadership program in Uganda, Families Mentoring Families (previous known as Enhance Humanity). The journey took 8 days and landed on the Okanagan heat wave that claimed 163 lives, reaching temperatures at 45C. These conditions forced Sav to run at midnight to avoid fatal temperatures. Unfortunately, on Day 7, Sav's heart began acting up from suspected heat-related issues, causing the attempt to be broken up into an additional week post the finish line. The efforts brought together an entire community of individuals, making headlines on the news and raising over $10,000 for the program. Sav fractured both feet on Day 5 and continued to run another 100KM on them. The pain caused symptoms of PTSD for Sav when she returned to running 6 months later. 

"It was - and honestly still is - the hardest thing I've ever done. I was not prepared for the amount of pain I would endure - I don't think there's anything I could've done to be ready for it. Not knowing how hard it would be was essential to even attempting it. But the kids kept me going. I have never experienced anything quite like it. You don't really consider your struggle when you're so arrested in the reason behind it. There's a quote about that - how our suffering ceases when it has a meaning. And this had such a powerful, potent, meaning for me. I wasn't doing it for bragging rights - I can guarantee I would've quit on Day 2 if that was my motive. Instead, I had something so real, so raw, so authentic driving me, that I endured hardship I don't actually think is physically possible to do again, and thrived. Cannot thank our community enough for their support, especially my Mom, who was with me from start to finish. She is my hero."

Theapeuo Thousand

On April 1 2022, Sav Holmes and a friend from Alaska, Kaylee Wilcox, cycled 1000KM for survivors of childhood sexual abuse through the UGrowGirl organization in Salmon Arm, BC.  

The 6 day expedition helped fund the #TimeToHeal retreat - a free healing retreat offered by Leha Marshall and Crystal Wood. 

The journey included several accidents, a windstorm and a knee injury that caused Kaylee to have to abandon the quest several KMs short. Through it all, the girls were able to connect with individuals throughout BC and share the important message of CSA. 

"I am so grateful for the opportunity to have cycled these 1000KMS for survivors. The Okanagan saw a 500% increase of CSA during COVID years, so it's something that is happening almost everyday. It might seem like the problem is too big to solve - and if we all do nothing, the answer is of course it is! But by breaking it down into small and simple efforts, a genuine change can occur. And that's what we saw here. Thank you to everyone who participated, donated, volunteered and supported - we have truly made a difference, and it's thanks to each of you!" 

- SAV

The Snow Run

Have you ever woken up days before your birthday and thought, "I should run 100KM to celebrate..." 

No? 

Well, I have. And that's how this challenge came to be. 

2021 was an incredible year for me, but I fell into a slight depression after the 500 Barefoot finished. All the excitement, the preparation, the focus and dedication leading up to it, disappeared almost overnight. And I wasn't ready for how that would feel. I began doubting myself and my abilities pretty massively after that experience and needed to shake myself out of the rut. 

Additionally, 2021 wasn't a very kind year to many of my friends. And I could see the defeat in the faces of my community, which made me want to do something crazy yet again, as a way of proving that anyone can do anythig they set their minds to. 

So, with absolutely 0 training, I headed out at 5AM in the dead of December and began running. A snowstorm blew in and made a reroute necessary to avoid being snow-ploughed off the road. A few dogs nearly got my behind for breakfast and black ice almost caused a hip replacement, but otherwise, the experience was one I enjoyed from start to finish. Snow blindness hit at the 65KM mark and I struggle to keep my eyes open after that, but we managed to press through the difficulty and successfully conquer 100KM in 13HRS. 

This effort was completely self-supported, with only handmade sandwiches and thrawed out water for nourishment. 

The Midnight Marathon

Life is about following the things that light you up. And the idea of running a marathon at midnight was playing on my mind for months. I love running in the dark as it adds an extra layer of focus, silence and stillness to the moment. 

After a full day of training - which included a 10KM run, a 3KM swim and a 50KM bike, I called a loyal adventurer friend (Gord) and we headed out. With no set goal in mind - other than finishing - I settled into a beautiful pace and felt amazing. By 3AM I was starting to feel the exhaustion set in, with eyelids getting heavy and my footsteps losing their spring. However, because the streets were empty of all people and sounds, it was easy to get into a powerful headspace and push through. That's when I began calculating the pace I'd need to hit a sub-4 marathon - and realized I was closer than expected. Turning on the fire, I accelerated the final 10KM and enjoyed a time of 3:57. 

While I packed everything from Gold Fish to sour peaches, I lived off of Huma Gels and caffeine pills instead. The result? Puking the second it was complete. 

The purpose of this expedition? To understand how my body would react to running while completey exhausted (in preparation for the Run4Rescue). Additionally, sometimes you just need to prove to yourself that you're an absolute badass - and this certainly did that! 

Kalmalka Lake Swim

On September 10TH 2023, Sav decided to swim Kalmalka Lake in the Okanagan - whiched totalled a 13KM crossing over 6HRS. With a fear of open water, it was more of a mental than physical challenge - although the rotator cuffs introduced a new level of pain to the game. An impromptu decision led to another limit broken: the fear of dark water. 

"In preparation for the Run4Rescue, I am practicing replicating experiences I'll likely encounter during those 25HRS. The cold and the boring(ness) of this challenge gave me a pretty clear picture of the struggle that lays ahead. I genuinely enjoy these adventures, but I have few positive things to say about this one. I was cold, in pain and just plain bored for the majority of it. With just me and my loyal kayaker on the water, it was a long and slow morning. Usually adrenaline kicks in and we get to enjoy some type of dopamine dump. But this experience was totally different. It was a real test of endurance, because it came with little satisification or joy." 

Big thanks to Gord for assisting with this crossing and to my continual sponsors who make these expeditions possible. A special S/O to Skratch Nutrition for being my drink of choice throughout this entire crossing - grateful to not have cramped once while using their electrolyte mixes!
